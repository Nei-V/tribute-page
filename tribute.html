Radio Free Europe / Radio Liberty

"The basis for RFE/RL's operations is that the first requirement of democracy is a well-informed citizenry. By promoting the free flow of information, RFE/RL supports the development of civil society and thus makes a contribution to long-term development and stability in the region we cover."

"I was beginning to realize that Europa Libera was more than just a radio outlet -- it was a crucial tool in speaking to Romanians. It had helped them not only stay informed, but also to stay sane and human."


  • 1949 - Radio Free Europe is formed
  • 1951 - 1956 - Using baloons to breach the Iron Curtain
  • 1951 - Radio Libery is formed
  • 1956 - Coverage of Hungarian Revolution
  • 1962 - Jamming during the Cuban Missile Crisis
  • 1965 - 1971 - Station infiltrated by Polish spies
  • 1972 - CIA stopped funding the stations
  • 1976 - Radio Free Europe and Radio Liberty merged
  • 1981 - RFE/RL Munich bombing
  • 1986 - Coverage of the Chernobyl disaster
  • 1989 - Coverage of the Velvet Revolution
  • 1991 - Coverage of the August Coup
  • 1993 - 2004 - Ceased broadcasting in most Eastern Europe
  • 2010 - Starts broadcasting in Pashtun tribal areas
